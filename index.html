Profile who I am, in brief.

Engineer working in statistical signal processing. Current focus on radar SP, estimation theory, spectral analysis, and the practical end of passive geolocation.

F / 01 Radar signal processing FDA, FMCW, pulse compression, beamforming. The algorithms that turn echoes into tracks.
F / 02 Statistical signal processing Detection, classification, denoising. Working with what the signal actually contains, not what it should.
F / 03 Estimation theory Linear and nonlinear parameter estimation, Cramer Rao bounds, Kalman and EKF filtering.
F / 04 Spectral analysis Time frequency methods, wavelet MRA, super-resolution, and the patterns that hide in band-limited signals.
F / 05 Passive geolocation TDoA, FDoA, multi-lateration, sensor fusion. Distributed asynchronous sensors, real-world constraints.

I work on the algorithms that turn radio frequencies and timing measurements into something useful. Emitter positions, target tracks, denoised waveforms. Most of what I build lives somewhere between a simulation, a signal processing chain, and an embedded system.

Current work at NASTP centers on passive geolocation. Earlier graduate research at the American University of Sharjah produced a published IEEE TAES paper on the effect of target motion on equivalent transmit beamforming for the Frequency Diverse Array. Marie Sklodowska-Curie Doctoral Networks fellowship awardee, 2023.

Experience roles, in reverse chronology.

  • Dec 2021 to Now

    DSP Simulation Engineer

    National Aerospace Science and Technology Park (NASTP)
    • Designed and implemented a Quadratically Constrained Least Squares (QCLS) Time-Difference-of-Arrival localization algorithm with iterative bias correction, EVD, Tikhonov regularization, and nonlinear optimization.
    • Built a two-step grid-search FDoA localization solver using Quasi-Newton optimization. RMSE of 200 m at a maximum range of 400 km, full computation in < 300 ms.
    • Developed a multi-lateration and sensor-fusion algorithm for distributed asynchronous passive sensors, hardware-integrated for full-scale prototyping (20 m to 1050 m accuracy at 400 km range).
    • Delivered an end-to-end radar simulator for SIL/HIL testing of RF front-end and signal processing chains for passive geolocation systems. Completed in under 11 months.
    • Integrated channel modeling (Rician, Rayleigh, Multipath, AWGN, TIREM, ITU stds.) and waveform synthesis (FMCW, CW, pulse, M-PSK, FSK, QAM) into the simulator.
    • Time-invariant wavelet denoising and wavelet MRA for OTA signal reconstruction. SNR improvements of up to 30%, timing accuracy under 100 ns.
    • Deployed and tested multi-lateration algorithms and a real-time tracker on UBX-160 embedded hardware.

    Graduate Research & Teaching Assistant

    American University of Sharjah, UAE
    • Reviewed contemporary Frequency Diverse Array (FDA) literature; identified lapses in fundamental assumptions for FDA used as a primary radar transceiver with equivalent transmit beamforming.
